# Citation Styles

## 1. In-Text Citation Formats

### APA 7th (Author–Date)
- **Parenthetical**: (Author, Year, p. Page) — e.g., (Smith, 2020, p. 45)
- **Narrative**: Author (Year) argues that... (p. 45)
- **Multiple authors**: (Smith & Jones, 2020); (Smith et al., 2020) for 3+ authors
- **No date**: (Smith, n.d.)
- **Multiple sources**: (Smith, 2020; Jones, 2019) — alphabetical, semicolon-separated

### Chicago 17th (Notes–Bibliography)
- **First citation**: Full note — Author First Last, *Title* (Place: Publisher, Year), Page.
- **Subsequent**: Short note — Last, *Short Title*, Page.
- **Ibid.** is no longer used; use shortened form instead.
- **No page number**: Cite chapter or section if available; otherwise omit.

### MLA 9th (Author–Page)
- **Parenthetical**: (Author Page) — e.g., (Smith 45)
- **Narrative**: Author argues that... (45)
- **Multiple authors**: (Smith and Jones 45); (Smith et al. 45) for 3+ authors
- **No page number**: Use author only or paragraph numbers: (Smith, par. 4)

### IEEE (Numeric)
- **In-text**: Bracket number — e.g., [1, p. 45] or [1]–[3]
- **Order**: First citation = [1], second = [2], etc.
- **Re-citation**: Use the original number; do not assign new numbers.

## 2. Bibliography / Works-Cited Formatting

| Style | Heading | Structure | Indentation |
|-------|---------|-----------|-------------|
| APA | References | Author, A. A. (Year). *Title*. Publisher. | Hanging |
| Chicago | Bibliography | Author Last, First. *Title*. Place: Publisher, Year. | Hanging |
| MLA | Works Cited | Author Last, First. *Title*. Publisher, Year. | Hanging |
| IEEE | References | [1] A. Author, *Title*. Publisher, Year. | Hanging |

**Ordering rules**: APA/MLA/IEEE — alphabetical by author surname. Chicago notes-bibliography — alphabetical or by first citation order (author's preference in the full note).

## 3. Key Differences Between Styles

- **APA**: Year appears immediately after author name. Page numbers in in-text citations only.
- **Chicago (NB)**: Year appears at end. Superscript footnote numbers in text. Full bibliographic details in first note.
- **MLA**: No year in in-text citations. Medium (Print, Web) no longer required in 9th ed.
- **IEEE**: Author names in initials-first format in references. No author name in in-text citations — only bracketed numbers.

## 4. Style Selection Guidelines

| Discipline | Preferred Style |
|------------|----------------|
| Psychology, Education, Social Sciences | APA 7th |
| History, Art History, Humanities | Chicago 17th (NB) |
| Literature, Languages, Cultural Studies | MLA 9th |
| Engineering, Computer Science | IEEE |
| Physical Sciences | ACS, CSE, or APA depending on journal |
| Law | Bluebook (ALWD) |

When a journal or publisher specifies a style, follow it strictly. When no style is specified, select based on disciplinary convention.

## 5. Common Citation Edge Cases

- **Multiple authors (6+ in APA)**: List first author + "et al." from first citation. Chicago lists up to 3; MLA lists up to 2.
- **No date**: Use "n.d." (APA), "n.d." in note (Chicago), or omit year (MLA).
- **Online sources**: APA requires DOI or URL. Chicago prefers DOI. MLA omits URLs for subscription databases.
- **Translations**: APA — (Author, Year/Original Year). Chicago — include "Trans." in note. MLA — include "Translated by."
- **Republished works**: Cite both original and republication dates: (Author, Original Year/Reprint Year).
- **Missing publisher location (Chicago)**: Use "n.p." or omit if unknown.

## 6. Practical Formatting Rules

- **Italics**: Book titles, journal names, volume numbers. Chapter/article titles in quotation marks (APA, MLA). Chicago uses italics for book titles, quotation marks for chapters.
- **Punctuation**: Periods after each major element. Commas between author, title, and publication data. APA uses a period after the year in references.
- **Ordering**: Alphabetize letter-by-letter (not word-by-word). "McCormack" before "MacDonald" in some styles — check style manual.
- **DOI vs URL**: Prefer DOI. Never cite a URL when a DOI exists. Remove "https://doi.org/" from APA DOI strings.
