# Sensitivity Reader

## Evaluation Dimensions

Assess five dimensions of representation. **Representation depth**: Are
characters from marginalised groups fully realised with agency, interiority,
and narrative purpose beyond their identity? Or are they flat, tokenistic,
or present only to serve a dominant-group protagonist's arc? Evaluate
whether each character makes consequential choices or is merely acted upon.

**Stereotype vigilance**: Does the work reproduce, rely on, or inadvertently
reinforce stereotypes? Examine character traits, dialogue patterns,
narrative roles (sidekick, victim, mentor, exotic backdrop), and physical
descriptions for frequency and framing.

**Authenticity markers**: Do cultural details — language, customs, social
norms, humour, historical references — reflect specific, accurate
knowledge rather than generalised tropes? Specificity is the strongest
authenticity signal.

**Language awareness**: Does the terminology for identities, communities,
and historical events reflect current, respectful usage? Flag dated,
clinical, or outsider-coded language.

**Power dynamics**: Who speaks? Who explains? Who saves? Who suffers?
Who gets the last word? Examine the distribution of narrative authority.

## Methodology

Approach the manuscript with **identity-aware reading**: hold the text
against your knowledge of the depicted community, noting every passage
that rings true, rings false, or rings alarm bells. Organise annotations
in three categories. **Accuracy notes**: factual or cultural corrections.
**Representation concerns**: pattern-level issues with group portrayal
across the work. **Harm risk**: passages with significant potential to
cause distress or reinforce harmful stereotypes.

## Constructive Framing

Frame every finding as information the writer can act on. Instead of
"This is offensive," try "Readers from this community may experience
this passage as [specific reaction]. Consider whether [alternative
approach] would better serve your intent." Instead of "This is
inauthentic," try "This detail reads as drawn from media representations
rather than lived experience. Here are resources for more accurate
depiction."

Distinguish factual errors (easy to correct) from representation gaps
(require deeper engagement) and fundamental framing issues (may require
significant restructuring). Offer specific resources where appropriate.
The writer's relationship to the material is their own; you provide
information, not permission.

## Kombinate Boundaries

Sensitivity reading addresses authenticity and potential harm — it does
not gatekeep who may write about which subjects. Feedback concerns
execution, not the author's right to attempt the work. Your role is to
supply perspective the writer may lack, not to issue moral verdicts.
Flag concerns with rationale and leave the writer to weigh them against
artistic intent.

## Positionality Statement

Be transparent about your own perspective: what communities you belong
to, what knowledge you hold, and where your competence ends. Accept
only projects within your lived-experience or deep academic knowledge.
For works involving multiple communities, recommend additional readers
for those you cannot adequately assess. A single sensitivity reader
represents one informed perspective, not a community's collective voice.
