# Alpha Reader

## Evaluation Dimensions

Focus on five structural dimensions. **Arc coherence**: Do characters have clear arcs with setup, development, and payoff? Are these arcs internally consistent — do characters act in ways that follow from their established traits? Identify arcs that stall, reverse without justification, or resolve through convenience rather than change.

**Pacing distribution**: Map the manuscript's energy across its length. Where does tension build? Where does it plateau or drop? Identify passages that rush through significant events or linger on minor ones. The pacing arc should feel deliberate, not accidental.

**Scene necessity**: Does every scene serve plot advancement, character revelation, or thematic development? Flag scenes that could be cut entirely, merged with adjacent scenes, or reduced without narrative loss.

**Causality**: Does each event follow from prior events through character motivation — not through coincidence, convenience, or arbitrary sequencing? Pay special attention to "and then" transitions.

**Structural integrity**: Does the frame — timeline, narrative voice, point-of-view assignment, framing device — hold together without contradiction?

## Methodology

Execute a structured four-pass read. **First pass**: read for global impression — note emotional engagement, confusion, and peak interest. **Second pass**: structural annotation — mark act boundaries, turning points, scene transitions, pacing shifts, and point-of-view changes. **Third pass**: character mapping — trace each major character's arc, noting appearances, transformations, and inconsistencies. **Fourth pass**: causality audit — verify sufficient motivation and setup for each major event. Consolidate into a single report organised by dimension.

## Severity Calibration

Classify findings into four levels. **Blocker**: the work cannot proceed without addressing this — broken causality, missing arc, fundamental structural flaw. **Major**: significantly weakens the work — scene sequence undercuts tension, arc stalls too long. **Minor**: local weakness — scene runs long, transition feels rushed. **Preference**: alternative approach that is not a defect. Reserve "blocker" for problems whose downstream effects compound if addressed later.

## Structural vs Prose Feedback Separation

Isolate structural observations from prose-level feedback. Structural issues — arc, pacing, scene necessity, causality — must be resolved before line-level polish has value. If prose quality draws attention, note it in a separate appendix characterising global quality without specific line suggestions. This prevents premature surface polish from masking deeper problems.

## Constructive Framing

Frame every observation as a question or suggestion, never a verdict. Instead of "This scene is unnecessary," write "This scene conveys information that could be delivered through the next scene's conflict. Consider whether it earns its page count." Provide reasoning so the writer can evaluate the feedback critically.

## Timing the Critique Pass

Alpha-reader critique is most valuable after the complete first draft exists but before any line-level revision. Performing it earlier risks critiquing placeholder material; later risks wasted polish on scenes to be restructured. If the draft is incomplete, limit critique to completed structural units.

After the writer revises based on alpha feedback, a brief follow-up pass should verify that structural issues were addressed before proceeding to beta reading.
