# Getting Started

Blank-page syndrome is a structural problem, not a creative one. If you do not know what to write, you do not have a spec yet. This skill provides strategies to generate that spec.

## Blank Page Syndrome Strategies

### Write the Worst Version First
Set a timer for 10 minutes. Write the worst possible version of the scene — cliché dialogue, purple prose, cardboard characters. You are not committing to this text; you are mapping the shape of the scene. Every awful draft reveals what the real version should be — reverse-image from the mistakes.

### Start in the Middle
Skip the opening. Write the scene you can visualise most clearly, regardless of where it falls in the chapter. Openings are best written last, once you know what the reader needs. Write scene 3 today; scenes 1-2 will become obvious once scene 3 exists.

### Write the Ending First
Write the last sentence of the chapter or section. Then write the sentence that comes right before it. Work backwards in reverse-outline fashion. Endings constrain beginnings — knowing where you land tells you where to start.

## Hook Types

A hook is the first thing the reader encounters. Choose a type that matches your track's tone:

| Hook Type | Example | Best For |
|-----------|---------|----------|
| **Situational** | A character in immediate physical jeopardy | Thrillers, action, suspense |
| **Character** | A distinctive voice or revealing action | Literary fiction, character-driven pieces |
| **Philosophical** | A provocative statement or question | Essays, non-fiction, speculative fiction |
| **Atmospheric** | Sensory immersion in setting | Literary fiction, atmospheric horror, travel |

## Story Seed Generators

- **What if inversion**: take a genre convention and invert one element. (What if the chosen one refuses? What if the detective is the murderer?)
- **Constraint play**: impose an arbitrary limit (first-person present tense, 50-word sentences, no adjectives) and write within it
- **Mashup**: combine two unrelated genres or settings (cyberpunk Regency romance, courtroom drama on a generational ship)

## Prompt Templates

> "The chapter needs to accomplish [goal]. The POV character is [character]. The previous chapter ended with [situation]. Begin drafting."
>
> "I have [concept / character / situation] but no scene. Generate 5 possible opening beats ranked by dramatic potential."
>
> "Write the first 100 words of this chapter in 3 different registers: formal literary, conversational, and pulpy. I will pick the direction."
>
> "I am stuck at [point in outline]. Diagnose what is blocking this scene — missing information, unclear motivation, wrong POV, or structural flaw — and unblock it."
